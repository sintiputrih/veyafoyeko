Top 10 Biggest LIV Golf Contracts

 Posted on March 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  547 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The emergence of LIV Golf, a breakaway tour backed by Saudi Arabia’s Public Investment Fund, has seen a surge in the earnings of the top golfers, with significant impacts on their contracts and overall financial standings.

1. Phil Mickelson - $138 million

  • On-course earnings: $102 million
  • Off-course earnings: $36 million
      • Mickelson's contract is estimated at a staggering $200 million guarantee, half of which is believed to have been received upfront. This substantial figure positions him as the highest-paid athlete in the world for 2022, surpassing renowned soccer star Lionel Messi. Mickelson's decision to join LIV Golf led to a temporary loss of sponsors and a self-imposed hiatus due to controversial comments about the PGA Tour and Saudi Arabia, further emphasizing the significance of his contract in reshaping the golfing landscape.

2. Dustin Johnson - $97 million

  • On-course earnings: $68 million
  • Off-course earnings: $29 million
    • Johnson's contract is estimated to include a $62 million signing bonus, contributing to his overall earnings, making him the fifth-highest-paid athlete globally.

3. Bryson DeChambeau - $86 million

  • On-course earnings: $66 million
  • Off-course earnings: $20 million
    • DeChambeau's contract is believed to exceed $125 million, with an estimated upfront payment of half this sum. This significant contract has positioned him ahead of NFL quarterback Tom Brady in earnings, showcasing the transformative effect of LIV Golf on golfers' financial profiles.

4. Brooks Koepka - $69 million

  • On-course earnings: $53 million
  • Off-course earnings: $16 million
    • Koepka's contract is estimated to have included a substantial $100 million guarantee from LIV, significantly contributing to his total earnings over the last year.

5. Tiger Woods - $68 million

  • On-course earnings: $43,500
  • Off-course earnings: $68 million
    • Despite playing sparingly due to his recovery from an accident, Woods turned down a substantial LIV offer in the "high nine digits," highlighting the impact of LIV Golf's significant financial incentives on top players.

6. Rory McIlroy - $43 million

  • On-course earnings: $9 million
  • Off-course earnings: $34 million
    • McIlroy's contract earnings reflect the financial consequences of his stance against LIV Golf, emphasizing the polarization within the golfing community and the financial implications for players.

7. Jordan Spieth - $39 million

  • On-course earnings: $8 million
  • Off-course earnings: $31 million
    • Spieth's on-course earnings have been affected by his support for the PGA Tour over LIV Golf, shedding light on the financial trade-offs associated with aligning with different golfing series.

8. Patrick Reed - $37 million

  • On-course earnings: $34 million
  • Off-course earnings: $3 million
    • Reed's transition to LIV Golf has seen a shift in his earnings profile, with a focus on sponsorships and the impact of series membership on his financial standing.

9. Sergio Garcia - $42 million

  • On-course earnings: $35 million
  • Off-course earnings: $7 million
    • Garcia's inflated on-course earnings illustrate the financial benefits of participating in LIV Golf, shedding light on the series' impact on golfers' financial rewards.

10. Charl Schwartzel - $34 million

  • On-course earnings: $30 million
  • Off-course earnings: $4 million
    • Schwartzel's success with LIV Golf has resulted in significant financial gains, underscoring the series' impact on golfers' financial prospects and reshaping the traditional golfing landscape.

The significant contracts and earnings of the top 10 LIV Golf players underscore the financial impact of the series, with substantial guarantees, bonuses, and on-course and off-course earnings reshaping the golfing landscape and players' financial prospects.
