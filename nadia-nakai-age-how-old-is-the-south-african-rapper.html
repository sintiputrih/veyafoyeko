Nadia Nakai Biography

Nadia Nakai, born of South African and Zimbabwean heritage, blossomed in Johannesburg.

After a stint at Fourways High School, Kenya called, completing her education.

In Kenya, rap’s heartbeat resonated in her soul.

Meeting Kenyan lyricist Nazizi sparked a passion, leading to her victory on ‘Shiz Niz’.

Her debut, “Like Me,” in 2013, marked her as a rising star.

2014 brought her to new heights, featuring on Riky Rick’s “Amantombazane” remix.

Cassper Nyovest recognized her talent, leading to a pivotal union with Family Tree Records.

Hits like “Saka Wena,” “The Man,” and “Naaa Meaan” followed.

2016 saw her EP “Bragga,” showcasing her versatility.

In 2019, “Nadia Naked” garnered acclaim and awards.

She was signed in 2020 by Def Jam Africa marking a skyrocket in her career in music.

In 2021, Channel O’s Gen-Z South Africa welcomed her as host. Leaving Family Tree Records, she founded Bragga Records.
