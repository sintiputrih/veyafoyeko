Aaron Rodgers Age, Height, Weight: Aaron Rodgers, officially known as Aaron Charles Rodgers is an American football player.

He developed a passion for playing American football at a younger age, has been consistent throughout his career, and has become one of the most sought-after American football players.

At this time of writing (Wednesday, 13 September 2023), Rodgers is an American football quarterback for the New York Jets of the National Football League.

Rodgers began his college football career at Butte College in 2002 before transferring to the University of California, Berkeley.

With the California Golden Bears, he set several career passing records, including lowest single-season and career interception rates

Rodgers was selected in the first round of the 2005 NFL Draft by the Green Bay Packers and played for the Packers from 2005 to 2022.

Rodgers became the Packers’ starting quarterback in 2008. In the 2010 season, he led them to a victory in Super Bowl XLV, earning the Super Bowl MVP.

He was named Associated Press Athlete of the Year in 2011 and was voted league MVP by the Associated Press for the 2011, 2014, 2020, and 2021 NFL seasons

He finished the 2022 season with 3,695 passing yards, 26 touchdowns, and 12 interceptions, which marked the most for Rodgers in a single season since 2008.

Rodgers spent his 18-year professional career with the Green Bay Packers until he asked for a trade in the off-season.

On April 26, 2023, Rodgers was traded to the New York Jets, along with the Packers’ first and fifth-round selections in the 2023 NFL Draft

Rodgers is regarded among the greatest and most talented quarterbacks of all time. He’s also a four-time winner of the Best NFL Player ESPY Award.

Apart from his career as an American football player, Rodgers has a minority ownership stake in the Milwaukee Bucks of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

In September 2023, Rodgers hit the headlines after suffering an injury on Monday, 11 September 2023 against the Buffalo Bills at MetLife Stadium on the 22nd anniversary of the September 11 attacks

The New York Jets quarterback will miss the rest of the NFL season with a torn Achilles tendon. The four-time Most Valuable Player, Rodgers sustained the injury just four snaps into his Jets debut.
