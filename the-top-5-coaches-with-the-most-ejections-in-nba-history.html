The Top 5 Coaches With the Most Ejections in NBA History

 Posted on September 24, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  550 words  |  Aldo Pusey

In the rich tapestry of NBA history, certain coaches have stood out not only for their coaching acumen and success but also for their fiery temperaments that sometimes led to ejections from games.

1. Red Auerbach

Career Highlights:

  • Coached the Boston Celtics to nine NBA championships, including a record eight consecutive titles from 1958 to 1966.
  • Inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame in 1969.
  • Mentored iconic players such as Bill Russell, Tommy Heinsohn, and Bob Cousy.

Ejections and Notable Incidents:

  • Auerbach was known for his fierce competitiveness, which often led to verbal altercations with officials, resulting in more fines and ejections than any other coach in NBA history.
  • He popularized the habit of smoking a victory cigar when he believed a game was already decided, a practice that gained cult-like popularity in the Boston area.

2. Larry Brown

Career Highlights:

  • Won an NBA championship with the Detroit Pistons in 2004.
  • Holds the record for the most regular-season victories in NBA history.
  • Coached several teams to successful seasons, including the Milwaukee Bucks, Golden State Warriors, and Dallas Mavericks.

Ejections and Notable Incidents:

  • Brown's ejections were often a result of his passionate and intense coaching style, which sometimes led to confrontations with officials.
  • Known for his ability to develop young talent and build solid defensive-minded teams.

3. Gregg Popovich

Career Highlights:

  • Guided the San Antonio Spurs to five NBA championships.
  • Known for his emphasis on team-first basketball and the development of a winning culture in San Antonio.
  • Has the most combined victories across the regular season and playoffs in NBA history.

Ejections and Notable Incidents:

  • Popovich's ejections were often a result of his vocal and animated interactions with officials, driven by his desire to advocate for his players and team.
  • Renowned for his ability to adapt his coaching style to the strengths of his players and for fostering a culture of accountability and selflessness within his teams.

4. Jack Ramsay

Career Highlights:

  • Coached the Portland Trail Blazers to their only NBA championship in 1977.
  • Led four different franchises to the playoffs, showcasing his adaptability and coaching prowess.
  • Inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame in 1992.

Ejections and Notable Incidents:

  • Ramsay's ejections were infrequent but often stemmed from his passionate demeanor and a commitment to defending his team's interests.
  • Known for his strategic coaching style and ability to maximize the potential of his players, evident in the Trail Blazers' championship run.

5. Pat Riley

Career Highlights:

  • Led the Los Angeles Lakers to four NBA championships and the Miami Heat to one.
  • Only coach to be selected as NBA Coach of the Year with three different franchises.
  • Inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame in 2008.

Ejections and Notable Incidents:

  • Riley's ejections were a reflection of his intense and competitive nature, often resulting from his vocal protests against officiating decisions.
  • Renowned for his innovative coaching methods and the ability to instill a winning mindset in his teams.

The top 5 coaches with the most ejections in NBA history have left an indelible mark on the league, not only for their successful coaching careers but also for their passionate and sometimes confrontational approach to the game.

Their legacies serve as a testament to the multifaceted nature of coaching in the NBA, where passion and intensity often collide with strategic acumen and leadership.
