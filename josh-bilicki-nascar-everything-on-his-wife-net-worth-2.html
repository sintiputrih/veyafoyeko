Josh Bilicki  Wikipedia

Josh Bilicki started with kid-karts at age four and quickly moved through the ranks to 80cc Shifter Karts. In 2011, he made the jump to 125cc Shifting Karts, and, in 2012, he was recognized and brought on board the Andrew Chase Racing-PCR team.

Josh is now competing in the Midwest Shifter Kart Series in a brand new PCR Shifter kart class.

Bilicki began racing sportscars at age 15, beginning with Spec Miata and the Sports Car Club of America (SCCA) in 2011. He earned his national license in 2012 and made his debut in an Area Sportsman Stock Car in 2012.

Josh’s first year of professional sportscar racing marked his first year in the IMSA Continental Tire Sportscar Challenge. He won two pole positions, four top-five finishing positions, and three podiums.

Bilicki debuted in the Xfinity Series with Obaika Racing at Road America in 2016. He made his Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series debut in June’s Toyota/Save Mart 350 at Sonoma Raceway. Bilicki finished 38th after a crash in qualifying and a dead battery during the race.

He made his NASCAR Gander Outdoors Truck Series debut in May.

The racer returned to the Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series in June, driving for Rick Ware Racing at Michigan. On February 4, 2019, it was announced that he would join RSS Racing to pilot the team’s No. 38 Chevrolet Camaro.
